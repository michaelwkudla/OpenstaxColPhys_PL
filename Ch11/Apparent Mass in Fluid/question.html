Archimedes principle can be used to calculate the density of a fluid as well as that of a solid. Suppose a chunk of Iron with a mass of {{params.m}} $g$ in air is found to have an apparent mass of {{params.mapp}} $g$ when completely submerged in an unknown liquid.
Part 1
What mass of fluid does the Iron displace?
Part 2
What is the volume of the Iron chunk, assuming that the average density of Iron is 7.8 $g/cm^3$?
Part 3
Calculate the density of the fluid.
