A block with mass ${{params.m}} kg$ resting on a frictionless surface is pushed with a constant force $F$. The acceleration of the block is found to be ${{params.a1}} m/s^2$.
Part 1
What is the force $F$ on the block?
Part 2
Now assume the surface is not frictionless, and that the surface has a static friction coefficient of ${{params.mu_s}}$. What pushing force is required to make the block slip?
Part 3
Assuming the block does slip, and a force of ${{params.F3}} N$ is applied. If the kinetic friction coefficient is ${{params.mu_k}}$, what is the acceleration of the block?
