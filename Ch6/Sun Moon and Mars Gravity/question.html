As we've learned in class, every object exerts a gravitational force on every other object. The sun, moon, and mars have respective masses of {{params.m_Sun}} $kg$, {{params.m_Moon}} $kg$, and {{params.m_Mars}} $kg$, and radii of {{params.r_Sun}} $km$, {{params.r_Moon}} $km$, and {{params.r_Mars}} $km$. Calculate the acceleration due to gravity at the surface of each celestial object described above. ** Note: Pay close attention to the units given and asked for!**
Part 1
Determine the acceleration due to the gravity of the Sun.
Part 2
Determine the acceleration due to the gravity of the Moon.
Part 3
Determine the acceleration due to the gravity of Mars.
